# Chase Sapphire Reserve: Your Guide to Business Class in Southern France, Barcelona, Florence & Austria

*How to turn Chase Ultimate Rewards points into lie-flat seats across the Atlantic — from LA and Orange County*

---

## The Big Picture: Why Transfers Beat the Portal

Your Chase Sapphire Reserve earns **Ultimate Rewards (UR) points**, which you can use two ways:

1. **Chase Travel Portal** — worth a flat 1.5 cents per point (100,000 pts = $1,500 in travel). Easy, but leaves a lot of value on the table for premium cabins.
2. **Transfer to airline partners** — at 1:1 ratio, your points become airline miles that can unlock business class seats worth **$4,000–$8,000+** at 60,000–80,000 miles. That's **2–4 cents per point** — double or triple the portal rate.

For business class to Europe, **always transfer. Never book through the portal.**

---

## Your Departure Airports: LAX and SNA

**SNA (John Wayne, Orange County)** has no international routes. Your options:
- **Drive to LAX** (~45 min without traffic) and originate your award there — most programs let you book LAX as the departure city.
- **Add a short domestic connection**: Some Aeroplan and Flying Blue awards will price SNA → LAX as a free or low-cost connection on the same ticket, keeping the overall cost at the LAX-to-Europe award rate. Call the airline after booking online to confirm.
- **Position on a cheap domestic ticket**: A Southwest or Alaska flight SNA–LAX costs $49–$79 and lets you access LAX's full menu of nonstop European business class flights.

For simplicity, all routes below are priced from **LAX**.

### 1. Southern France — Nice (NCE) or Marseille (MRS)

**Gateway airports:** Nice Côte d'Azur (NCE) is the best hub for the Riviera, Provence, and the Côte d'Azur. Marseille (MRS) works for Provence and the western Languedoc coast.

#### Best Redemption: Flying Blue (Air France/KLM)
Air France flies **LAX → Paris CDG → NCE** and **LAX → Paris CDG → MRS** daily. Business class on the transatlantic leg (La Business) features fully flat seats, direct aisle access, and exceptional French service. The CDG–NCE connection is typically in economy (1.5 hrs), though you can sometimes upgrade or book business separately.

- **Cost:** ~55,000–65,000 Flying Blue miles one-way (dynamic pricing)
- **Monthly Promo Rewards:** Flying Blue runs monthly discounts — in recent months, LAX–Europe business class has appeared as low as **45,000 miles**. Check flyingblue.com on the 1st of each month.
- **Booking tip:** Use the Flying Blue calendar view to find lowest-priced dates before transferring points.

> **Act Now — 20% Transfer Bonus:** Through **May 27, 2026**, Chase is offering a **1:1.2 transfer ratio** to Flying Blue. Transfer 50,000 UR → get 60,000 Flying Blue miles. This is one of the best transfer bonuses available right now.

#### Alternative: Aeroplan (via Lufthansa/SWISS)
Book **LAX → Frankfurt (FRA) or Zurich (ZRH) → Nice** on Lufthansa or SWISS in business class. Lufthansa's long-haul business class features fully flat seats.

- **Cost:** ~60,000–70,000 Aeroplan points one-way (pre-June 1, 2026 rates; after that, expect 75,000)
- **Book before June 1, 2026** to lock in current pricing.

### 2. Barcelona (BCN)

**Gateway airport:** Barcelona El Prat (BCN).

#### Best Redemption: Iberia Plus Avios — the Only Nonstop From LAX
Iberia is the **only airline with a nonstop LAX–Barcelona route**. The flight operates on Iberia's A330 in Businesss class ("Business Plus"), featuring fully flat beds and direct aisle access.

- **Off-peak cost:** ~**40,500 Avios** one-way (off-peak dates: generally Jan–Mar, Oct–Nov)
- **Peak cost:** ~50,000–55,000 Avios one-way
- Iberia awards have **no fuel surcharges** on Iberia-operated flights — you pay only ~$50–$80 in taxes.
- Transfer Chase UR → Iberia Plus at 1:1. Points post within 1–3 business days.
- **Booking:** iberia.com → "Use Avios" → select "Avios Redemption" → search LAX–BCN.

> **Note:** Iberia increased award rates ~19% in 2025. The off-peak sweet spot (formerly 34,000 Avios) is now 40,500 Avios. Still one of the best business class deals to Europe on a nonstop flight.

#### Alternative: Flying Blue (Air France via CDG)
**LAX → CDG → BCN** on Air France business class, then a 2-hour short-haul hop to Barcelona.
- **Cost:** ~55,000–65,000 Flying Blue miles, or less during Promo Rewards months.
- With the current 20% Chase transfer bonus, this is very competitive.

### 3. Florence (FLR / Florence Peretola)

**Gateway airports:** Florence (FLR) is a small regional airport with no transatlantic flights. Your best options:
- **Rome Fiumicino (FCO)** → high-speed Frecciarossa train to Florence: **1.5 hours**, ~€25–€50.
- **Milan Malpensa (MXP)** → high-speed train to Florence: **2 hours**, ~€30–€60.
- **Paris CDG** → Air France flight to Florence (FLR) exists but is short-haul economy only.

The train option from Rome is widely considered the most elegant approach — it's scenic, fast, and Rome itself is worth a night.

#### Best Redemption: Flying Blue (LAX → CDG → FCO, then train)
Air France flies **LAX → CDG** in La Business (lie-flat), then connect to **CDG → FCO** on Air France (shorter flight, often in economy). Take the Frecciarossa from Rome Termini to Florence Santa Maria Novella.

- **Cost:** ~55,000–65,000 Flying Blue miles one-way (to FCO)
- Business class on the transatlantic segment; economy on the CDG–FCO hop.

#### Alternative: Aeroplan (LAX → FRA or ZRH → FCO or MXP)
Book on Lufthansa or SWISS in business class all the way to Italy. Lufthansa flies FRA → FCO and FRA → MXP with connecting service from LAX.

- **Cost:** ~60,000–70,000 Aeroplan points one-way (book before June 1, 2026 for lower rates)
- SWISS flies ZRH → MXP for easy onward connection.

#### Alternative: Aeroplan Stopover — See Two Cities for Almost No Extra Points
Book **LAX → Rome (FCO) → [5-day stopover] → Florence (FLR)** on a single Aeroplan award. The stopover costs only **+5,000 points**. Spend time in Rome, then fly or train to Florence — remarkable value for exploring two iconic Italian cities.

### 4. Austria — Vienna (VIE)

**Gateway airport:** Vienna International (VIE).

#### Best Redemption: Aeroplan on Austrian Airlines
Austrian Airlines is a **Lufthansa Group** member and bookable via Aeroplan. LAX → Vienna on Austrian features outstanding long-haul business class (flat beds, exceptional Austrian food and wine service).

- **Cost:** ~**70,000 Aeroplan points** one-way (current rates; increasing to ~75,000 after June 1, 2026)
- No fuel surcharges via Aeroplan on Austrian-operated flights.
- **Book before June 1, 2026** to save 5,000 points per ticket.
- **Routing:** LAX → VIE nonstop on Austrian (12 hrs), or LAX → FRA → VIE on Lufthansa.

> Austrian Airlines' business class on widebody aircraft is a hidden gem — warm, attentive service, excellent Austrian cuisine, and a quieter cabin than many competing products.

#### Alternative: Flying Blue (via Amsterdam or Paris)
KLM flies **LAX → AMS → VIE** with business class on the transatlantic segment.

- **Cost:** ~55,000–65,000 Flying Blue miles one-way.
- With the current 20% Chase transfer bonus, this can be very competitive.

## Key Strategies & Pro Tips

### 1. Use the Flying Blue 20% Transfer Bonus Now
Through **May 27, 2026**, Chase UR transfers to Flying Blue at **1:1.2**. If you're targeting Southern France, Barcelona, or Florence via Paris, transfer your points now while this bonus is active. Points don't expire as long as you have some account activity.

### 2. Book Aeroplan Before June 1, 2026
Aeroplan is raising partner award rates by up to 25% on June 1, 2026. For Austria and any Lufthansa/SWISS redemptions, booking before that date saves 5,000–10,000 points per ticket.

### 3. The Aeroplan Stopover Trick for Multi-City Europe Trips
If you want to visit multiple destinations — say, Vienna **and** Florence — Aeroplan's stopover program is unmatched. For just **+5,000 points**, you can add a multi-day stop in one city before continuing to your final destination on the same award ticket. Example:

```
LAX → VIE [5-day stopover in Vienna]
     → FCO (Rome, then train to Florence)
Total cost: ~75,000 Aeroplan points one-way
```

This is extraordinary value — two European cities on one award ticket.

### 4. Watch Flying Blue Promo Rewards Monthly
On the **1st of each month**, Flying Blue releases its Promo Rewards: discounted business class awards (typically 25–30% off). LAX–Europe business class often appears at **45,000–50,000 miles** during promo months. Don't transfer until you confirm award availability and pricing.

### 5. Iberia Off-Peak Windows for Barcelona
If Barcelona is your target, book **Iberia Plus Avios** during off-peak dates for the lowest rates (~40,500 Avios). Off-peak periods generally cover:
- January through mid-March
- October and November

Summer and holiday travel is peak-priced; plan shoulder-season travel for the best value.

### 6. Combine Programs for Multi-Destination Trips
You can use **different programs for different segments** — there's no rule requiring you to use the same currency for every flight. Example for a 3-week itinerary:

- **Leg 1:** LAX → BCN on Iberia Plus (40,500 Avios, nonstop)
- **Travel overland:** Barcelona → Nice by train (~5.5 hrs, scenic)
- **Leg 2:** NCE → LAX on Air France via Flying Blue (55,000 miles, via CDG)

Total cost: ~95,500 points, covering two premier European destinations in business class.

## Step-by-Step: How to Book

1. **Check award availability first** — search on the airline's website before transferring any points. Flying Blue: flyingblue.com | Aeroplan: aircanada.com | Iberia: iberia.com
2. **Confirm the routing and date you want** — note the flight numbers and dates.
3. **Transfer your Chase UR points** — go to chase.com → Ultimate Rewards → Transfer Points → select your airline partner → transfer exact amount needed.
4. **Wait for transfer to post** — Flying Blue and Aeroplan are near-instant; Iberia can take 1–3 business days.
5. **Book the award** — return to the airline website and book using your now-loaded miles.

> **Important:** Award availability can disappear between when you find it and when your points post. For popular routes and dates, call the airline's award booking line immediately after transferring to hold the seat while points arrive.

## The Bottom Line

For luxury travel to these four European destinations, your Chase Sapphire Reserve points have a clear hierarchy:

- **Barcelona** → Iberia Plus, nonstop LAX–BCN, off-peak dates. The cleanest redemption available.
- **Southern France** → Flying Blue, especially with the current 20% transfer bonus. Check monthly Promo Rewards.
- **Florence** → Flying Blue to Rome or Aeroplan to Milan, then train. The stopover trick adds great value.
- **Austria** → Aeroplan on Austrian Airlines. Book before June 1, 2026 before rates increase.

The portal is a fallback, not a strategy. Transfer your points, book lie-flat seats, and experience Europe the way it should be experienced — arriving rested, in style.
